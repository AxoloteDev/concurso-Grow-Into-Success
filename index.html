Why Don’t We Accomplish the Goals We Set for Ourselves?

Why do we have this dream in our head of who we could be, but when we set out to change and create a new identity for ourselves we fail?

Breaking out of a toxic relationship…

Losing weight, gaining muscle, or making any other positive health change…

Learning a new skill, transitioning careers, finally starting that business you’ve always dreamt about…

We know these things are good for us. We might commit to change and the first week or two go smoothly… But then, something happens. We miss a day. We tell ourselves that it’s OK and we’ll get back to it tomorrow. But then another day passes by. And another. And eventually we’re back exactly to where we started feeling guilty, frustrated, and hopeless.

Are we destined to just repeat this endless cycle forever?

NO!

But You Have to Understand WHY This Cycle Happens in the First Place.

This is best demonstrated with a question

When you go to a restaurant and choose what to eat, who controls what you eat?

You might think it’s you, but that’s not exactly correct.

You can CHOOSE from the options on the menu, but ultimately the restaurant owner is the one who controls what you eat.

You can’t get spaghetti and meatballs if you go to a Japanese restaurant and vice versa.

The Same Idea Applies to Your Conscious and Subconscious Mind

While you might consciously “decide” that you want to do something, if that option isn’t on your subconscious menu, no amount of willpower will be enough.

Our conscious mind might desire change and know that life would be so much better “if only I could do THIS.”

But if your subconscious mind isn’t aligned, it will eventually sabotage you - no matter how bad you want this change to happen.

To top it off, even if your conscious and subconscious are aligned, if you’re following the wrong process for change - it’s still unlikely you’ll succeed.

There’s a step by step process you MUST go through to create a transformation in any area of your life, but most courses and self-help books stop halfway. They make you feel like change is possible, but don’t show you how to keep going once you encounter resistance after breaking out of your comfort zone.

So what is this process?

It’s Called the LIGHT Method

The first step is:

LEARN

You have to get knowledge to get started. This is pretty obvious if you’ve ever tried to change anything in your life.

But just learning isn’t enough. I’m sure you know someone who is a lifelong learner or “know it all,” but their knowledge is just that… knowledge. They’ve never done the thing they’ve learned about.

That’s why the next step is critical.

IMPLEMENT

Without implementing what you’ve learned, that knowledge is useless.

The trick is implementing it in a very deliberate way. Not doing too much so you don’t burn yourself out, but also not doing too little so you don’t fall back to square one.

Just “dabbling” isn’t enough. You have to commit yourself which will lead you into the next stage:

GROW

When you’ve made it through the first two stages – learning and implementing – you may begin to see some results and improvements in your life.

But this is where most people get stuck and where most self-help programs stop.

I’m sure you know someone in your life (I’m one of them, I’ll admit) who decided to become more physically active or lose weight.

They commit to a diet, start exercising, and maybe even start seeing some results…

But a few months later you see them again and they’ve gained back the weight and gone back to their old eating habits.

This happens because they never go past this grow stage. They get stuck in this stage and eventually go back to their old way of doing things and stay stagnant.

That’s why the next stage is so crucial if you want to create lasting change.

HUSTLE

You have to let the brain know you’re in charge.

If you don’t know how your mind works, you might follow your brain’s commands to go back to what’s comfortable, but you can take control.

You can show your mind that you’re serious about the transformation and eventually it will accept it.

This is where you transition from purely using willpower to re-shaping your identity to create the new you. A new you for whom the better behavior is as easy as breathing.

This can be an intense and difficult stage.

A lot of positive and negative emotions can show up here. Stepping out of your comfort zone can feel uncomfortable, and your brain will actually perceive these changes as pain and will try to keep you away from growing.

But this won’t last forever and you’ll reach the final stage of your transformation sooner than you might think.

THRIVE

Your brain has created new neural pathways.

What once was scary and unknown becomes familiar and comfortable.

You’ve created a new identity. What previously seemed impossible will now become effortless and your mind, brain, and body are all aligned to work together in the same direction - the exact direction you want to be heading in.

Now, this is all great…

But How Do You Actually Do All of This?

How do you implement the LIGHT method and address your subconscious at the same time?

How do you create a life where you’re excited to wake up every morning to take on the day?

And how do you create this identity shift that makes even the hardest tasks become effortless?

It all starts with becoming your best self - which is exactly what this course will help you do.
